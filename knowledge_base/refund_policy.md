# Refund Policy & Customer Retention Playbook

## Overview
This document covers our refund eligibility rules, the exception process, credit vs cash refund decisions, and the retention playbook for at-risk customers. Customer success and support teams should reference this document before responding to any refund or cancellation request.

---

## Standard Refund Policy

### Core Rule
**Refunds are not available after 14 days from the date of purchase or renewal.**

### Within 14 Days
- Full refund available, no questions asked
- Refund processed within 5-7 business days to original payment method
- Account access terminated upon refund processing
- To request: email billing@company.com with subject "Refund Request — [Account Email]"

### After 14 Days
- Cash refunds are not available as standard policy
- Account credits may be offered at customer success discretion
- Credits are applied to future invoices
- Credits valid for 12 months from issue date

### Annual Plans
- Annual plans cancelled within 14 days: full cash refund
- Annual plans cancelled after 14 days: remaining months issued as account credit (not cash)
- Example: Customer on $1,428/year plan cancels at month 4 → 8 months credit = $952 credit

## Refund Exception Process

In certain circumstances, exceptions to the 14-day rule may be approved.

### Grounds for Exception
1. **Product failure:** Documented platform bugs that prevented core functionality for >48 hours
2. **Billing error:** Duplicate charges, incorrect plan charged, unauthorized charges
3. **Misrepresentation:** Feature was promised by sales that does not exist in product
4. **Medical or bereavement:** Customer provides documentation
5. **Chatbot or agent misinformation:** Customer was given incorrect policy information by our AI systems

### Exception Approval Authority
- Support agents: can approve credits up to $100
- Customer success managers: can approve credits up to $500 or cash refunds up to $200
- Head of Customer Success: can approve any amount
- CFO approval required for cash refunds over $1,000

### How to Process an Exception
1. Document the customer's complaint and the specific ground for exception
2. Verify the claim (check platform logs for outages, review sales call recordings if misrepresentation claimed)
3. Escalate to appropriate approval authority
4. Communicate decision within 2 business days of exception request
5. Log the exception in the CRM with reason and approval chain

## Credits vs Cash Refunds

### When to Offer Credits
- Customer wants to continue using the platform
- Issue was a one-time incident, not a systemic problem
- Refund request is after 14 days but customer has valid grievance
- Customer is at churn risk but can be retained

### When to Offer Cash Refunds
- Customer is within 14-day window
- Billing error (always cash)
- Customer explicitly states they will not continue using the platform
- Approved exception with documented product failure

### Credit Offer Guidelines
- Standard retention credit: 1 month free (value = current monthly plan cost)
- High-value customer (>$500/month): up to 2 months free credit
- VIP customer or at churn risk: escalate to Customer Success Manager for custom offer
- Do not offer more than 3 months credit without Head of CS approval

## Retention Playbook

Use this playbook when a customer has expressed dissatisfaction, requested a refund, threatened to cancel, or threatened to post public reviews.

### Step 1: Acknowledge Immediately
- Response must go out within 4 hours of receiving a complaint
- Never let a complaint go unanswered for more than 24 hours
- If 3+ emails have gone unanswered: treat as critical escalation regardless of content

### Step 2: Assess the Customer
Check the CRM for:
- Account value (monthly/annual spend)
- Tenure (how long they've been a customer)
- VIP status
- Number of seats / team size
- Previous complaints or credits issued
- Churn risk score

### Step 3: Match Response to Risk Level

#### Low Risk (account value <$200/month, first complaint)
- Empathetic acknowledgement
- Offer to investigate the issue
- 1-week timeline to resolve
- Offer 1 month credit if issue is confirmed

#### Medium Risk (account value $200-$500/month, or 2nd complaint)
- Personal response from Customer Success Manager (not support agent)
- Acknowledgement call within 24 hours if possible
- Offer 1 month free credit immediately as goodwill
- Commit to resolution timeline in writing

#### High Risk (account value >$500/month, or review threat, or 3+ unanswered emails)
- Escalate to Head of Customer Success immediately
- Personal outreach from CS Manager within 2 hours
- Offer 2 months free credit + dedicated support contact
- If performance issue: expedite fix to engineering, give customer direct update
- If public review already posted: coordinate with marketing on response

#### Critical Risk (VIP customer, >$1,000/month, legal threat, or public review already posted)
- Escalate to VP of Customer Success and CEO awareness
- Same-day personal call from senior leadership
- Custom retention offer (may include cash refund, extended free period, custom feature development commitment)
- Legal team notified if legal language used

### Step 4: The Retention Offer Script

Use this framework for retention conversations:

> "Thank you for bringing this to our attention. I want to personally apologize for [specific issue]. This is not the experience we want you to have. To make this right, I'd like to offer [specific offer]. I'm also personally committed to ensuring [specific resolution] happens by [specific date]. Can we schedule a quick call to discuss?"

### Step 5: Follow Up
- Send written confirmation of any offer made
- Log the interaction in CRM with full details
- Follow up within 48 hours of offer to confirm acceptance
- If customer accepts: mark as "Retained" in CRM, set 30-day check-in reminder
- If customer declines and churns: send exit survey, log churn reason

## Karen's Scenario — Specific Guidance

When a customer has sent 3+ emails with zero replies and is threatening public reviews:

1. This is an **automatic High Risk escalation** regardless of account value
2. The lack of response is our failure — acknowledge this explicitly
3. Do NOT make excuses for the non-response
4. Offer: 1 month free credit + immediate investigation of the performance issue
5. If performance issue is confirmed: offer additional credit per the SLA policy
6. Assign a named Customer Success Manager as their dedicated contact
7. Trigger web intelligence check: has the review already been posted?
8. If review already posted: coordinate marketing response within 24 hours

## Chatbot / AI Misinformation Policy

If a customer was given incorrect information by our AI chatbot or agent:

1. **Do not deny** that the misinformation occurred — check logs to confirm
2. **Do not admit legal liability** — use language like "I want to clarify the correct policy"
3. Acknowledge the confusion: "I understand why that would be confusing given what you were told"
4. State the correct policy clearly and specifically
5. If customer made a financial decision based on incorrect info: escalate to Head of CS
6. Offer credit as goodwill for the confusion caused
7. File an internal ticket for the AI/product team to fix the chatbot response

**Correct framing:** "I want to make sure you have accurate information about our refund policy..."
**Incorrect framing:** "Our chatbot made an error and we apologize for the mistake..." (admits liability)

## Cancellation Process

### How Customers Cancel
- Self-serve: Settings → Billing → Cancel Subscription
- Via support: email billing@company.com
- For Enterprise: must notify account manager 30 days in advance per contract

### What Happens on Cancellation
- Access continues until end of current billing period
- Data export available for 30 days after cancellation
- After 30 days: data permanently deleted (GDPR compliant)
- Reactivation possible within 30 days with full data restoration

### Win-Back (Churned Customers)
- Churned customers who return within 90 days: offer 1 month free on any plan
- Churned customers who return after 90 days: standard pricing applies
- If customer churned due to a product issue that has since been fixed: proactive outreach with update
